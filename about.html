About IBCOL

The International Blockchain Olympiad is an annual student competition that challenges students to seek out a real-world problem that could be solved with a blockchain-enabled solution.
The objective of the competition is to provide students and young professionals for correct and complete blockchain solutions, the opportunity to showcase their knowledge and abilities in blockchain technology, gain valuable experience, and potentially win prizes or recognition for their work.
International Blockchain Olympiad was founded in 2017. Quietly growing into its 6th year, the International Blockchain Olympiad hosts its 2023 finals in Amsterdam.

About Blockchain Technology

Blockchain technology is a decentralized, digital ledger that enables trust and transparent transactions without the need for a central authority. It was initially introduced in 2008 as the underlying technology for the cryptocurrency, Bitcoin. The key advantage of blockchain lies in its ability to facilitate efficient and trustworthy collaborations among parties that may not necessarily trust one another, making it an ideal solution for decentralized applications. Although decentralization is one of the primary benefits of blockchain technology, it is not the sole advantage it offers.

About Blockchain Solutions

A blockchain-enabled solution leverages blockchain as a technology as well as privacy enhancing technologies to provide a single truth for an ecosystem of stakeholders that may otherwise not be able to cooperate and collaborate, due to the cost of enabling and maintaining that single truth in a world where trust is scarce by nature. Blockchain-enabled solutions provide a zero-trust environment.

About Blockchain Careers

There are a variety of careers available in the field of blockchain technology. Here are some examples:

1. Blockchain Developer: This role involves designing, developing, and implementing blockchain-based solutions. This may include writing code, creating smart contracts, and integrating blockchain technology with other systems.
2. Blockchain Architect: Similar to a software architect, a blockchain architect is responsible for designing the overall structure and functionality of a blockchain system. This involves understanding the technical requirements of the project and working with other stakeholders to ensure that the architecture meets their needs.
3. Blockchain Business Analyst: This role involves analyzing business requirements and identifying opportunities for blockchain-based solutions. A blockchain business analyst must have a deep understanding of both blockchain technology and the industry in which they are working.
4. Blockchain Project Manager: This role involves overseeing the development and implementation of blockchain-based projects. A blockchain project manager must be able to manage multiple stakeholders and ensure that the project is delivered on time and within budget.
5. Blockchain Marketing and Communications Specialist: This role involves promoting the benefits of blockchain technology to potential clients and stakeholders. A blockchain marketing and communications specialist must be able to translate technical concepts into language that is accessible to a non-technical audience.

These are just a few examples of the many careers available in the blockchain industry. As blockchain technology continues to grow and evolve, there will likely be even more opportunities for individuals with specialized skills and knowledge in this field.

Sponsors & Supporting Organisations

By working together, these partners have helped to support IBCOL to drive growth and promote the wider adoption of blockchain technology at international level.
